Explore the Porsche 911 lineup

The Porsche 911 is not just a car — it's an icon. Over decades, it has evolved without losing its soul. Today's 911s are faster, sharper, and more advanced, yet they stay true to the spirit of the very first 911 in 1964. Let's meet the legends that define this era of Porsche performance.

The Porsche engineers knew exactly what their objective was. To make the 911 Porsche the most powerful car for the road, objective achieved. The water-cooled six cylinder by Tauber Boxster engine with four valve technology, Variable Turbine Geometry(VTG), delivers its power through the rear axle. From its 3.6L capacity, it generates an enormous speed 620 horsepower at an egine speed of 6500RPM and 700 newton meters between 2250 and 5500RPM.
